Amphibians are a vital part of our ecosystem—they are indicators of the environment and important predators for many annoying pests. The whole class of amphibians is one of the most ancient lineages stretching back to the dawn of time, when the first vertebrates crawled from the shallow seas onto land. This website focuses on the amphibians native to Wisconsin. Take a moment to discover these fascinating creatures in our very own backyard!

Amphibians often are saddled with negative stereotypes of being, dirty, slimy, and gross. Some species of frogs can fully breathe underwater by pulling oxygen straight from the water to supplement their lungs. Some species live their entire lives underground only going to water to spawn the next generation. Most amphibians go through a process called metamorphosis. This is a process where an organism changes from their adolescent form, to their mature adult form. This is one of the many things that make these slimy little friends a fascinating part of our amazing world.
